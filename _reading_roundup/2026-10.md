Welcome to the first installment of Reading Roundup — where I plan to collect stories, essays, and research that caught my attention over the last week. The focus is going to be pretty broad — primarily on technology, with a pull toward security, offensive research, and parts of the internet and technology stack we pay the least attention to. I pay close attention to the broader national security, military, and political arenas — so there will be some of that as well.

No exhaustive coverage; no hot takes for their own sake; just things I found worth reading, sharing, and filing away.

---

**[A Possible US Government iPhone-Hacking Toolkit Is Now In the Hands of Foreign Spies, Criminals](https://www.wired.com/story/coruna-iphone-hacking-toolkit-us-government/)**
Google traced a 5-chain, 23-exploit iOS kit from a government surveillance vendor to Russian espionage operations in Ukraine, then to Chinese financial criminals. EternalBlue all over again — government-grade tools loose in the wild, moving through a secondhand zero-day market nobody fully tracks.

**[CBP Tapped Into the Online Advertising Ecosystem To Track Peoples' Movements](https://www.404media.co/cbp-tapped-into-the-online-advertising-ecosystem-to-track-peoples-movements/)**
An internal DHS document obtained via FOIA confirms CBP bought ad-industry location data — pulled from games, dating apps, fitness trackers — to track phone movements over time. Add to my growing list of reasons I hate big-tech driven surveillance capitalism — this is just another example, with an uncomfortable crossover with government use.

**[On the Coming Industrialisation of Exploit Generation with LLMs](https://sean.heelan.io/2026/01/18/on-the-coming-industrialisation-of-exploit-generation-with-llms/)**
The author ran agents against a zero-day in QuickJS with modern mitigations in place; they produced 40+ working exploits across six scenarios. The conclusion: the limiting factor on offensive cyber is shifting from headcount to token throughput.

**[How Vulnerable Are Computers to an 80-Year-Old Spy Technique?](https://www.wired.com/story/how-vulnerable-are-computers-to-an-80-year-old-spy-technique-congress-wants-answers/)**
Congress asked the GAO to look at TEMPEST-style side-channel attacks — reading data from the electromagnetic and acoustic emissions devices produce. The reason it's relevant now: AI can reconstruct screen content from EM leakage at 70%+ accuracy, which moves this from theoretical to worth worrying about.

**[A Hard Truth in Munich: Cyber Defense Runs Through Silicon Valley](https://therecord.media/munich-silicon-valley-cyber-defense)**
Cyberattacks ranked as the top perceived security risk in G7 countries at Munich 2026. The uncomfortable undercurrent: Western defense infrastructure runs on Silicon Valley commercial cloud, and nobody has a clean answer for what that dependency costs — see the DoD / Anthropic fallout as an interesting, tangential case study.

**[The Software Industrial Revolution](https://cannoneyed.com/essays/software-industrial-revolution)**
Interesting framing that AI is to software what mechanization was to manufacturing — the thing that makes abundant what was previously scarce and expensive.

**[When the Model Is the Machine](https://blog.mikegchambers.com/posts/when-the-model-is-the-machine/)**
The author builds a working app with no codebase — an AI agent generates it at runtime from a URL prompt — then uses it to ask what that actually means for SaaS and the role of the developer moving forward.

**[In Defense of Not Reading the Code](https://www.benshoemaker.us/writing/in-defense-of-not-reading-the-code/)**
When three engineers produce a million lines of AI-generated code, reading it isn't the job anymore — building the harness around it is.

**[The End of CI/CD Pipelines: The Dawn of Agentic DevOps](https://hackernoon.com/the-end-of-cicd-pipelines-the-dawn-of-agentic-devops)**
Static pipelines are giving way to agents that reason about what needs to happen and adapt mid-run. More directional than technical, but a useful frame for where DevOps is heading.

**[Subscriptions Will Survive in Exactly Two Places](https://productics.substack.com/p/subscriptions-will-survive-in-exactly)**
The subscription model spread far beyond where it makes sense, and (hopefully) the correction is underway.
